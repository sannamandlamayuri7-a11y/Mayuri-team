General Description


general description is a brief overview that explains the main idea or basic information about a topic without going into detailed explanations.
Definition
A general description presents the overall features, purpose, and important points of a subject in simple terms so that the reader can quickly understand what the topic is about.
Features of a General Description
Gives basic introduction to the topic
Includes main characteristics or purpose
Avoids deep technical details
Usually short and easy to understand

Novelty / Uniqueness:


Novelty refers to the newness or originality of an idea, product, method, or research.
It shows that something has not been done before or introduces a new approach, concept, or improvement compared to existing methods.
Example:
Using AI-based sensors to automatically control rainwater harvesting systems is considered novel because it introduces a new technology-based method.

Business / Social Impact:


Business impact refers to how a product, idea, or project affects economic activities, industries, or companies.
Creates new business opportunities and startups
Generates employment opportunities
Increases profitability and market growth
Reduces operational costs through efficient methods
Encourages innovation and competition in the market

Technology Architecture:


technology stack (tech stack) is the combination of programming languages, frameworks, tools, software, and hardware used to develop and run a project, application, or system.
It describes what technologies are used at different levels of development.
Main Layers of a Technology Stack
Frontend (User Interface)
Technologies used to design what users see and interact with.
Examples: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Angular
Backend (Server Side)
Technologies that handle logic, processing, and communication with databases.
Examples: Python, Java, Node.js, PHP

Scope of the Work:


Scope of work refers to the range of activities, tasks, objectives, and responsibilities that will be completed in a project.
It clearly defines what work will be done, how it will be done, and what results are expected.
Key Elements of Scope of Work
Project objectives – what the project aims to achieve
Tasks and activities – specific work to be performed
Resources required – materials, tools, manpower, and technology
Time frame – duration and schedule of the project
Expected outcomes – final deliverables or results
